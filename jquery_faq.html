National Park FAQs

10 FAQs about National Parks:

How many national parks are there?
There are currently 63 national parks.
What are the top 3 most visited national parks?
Great Smoky Mountains National Park
Yellowstone National Park
Zion National Park
Who is in charge of the national parks?
The National Park Service.
When was the National Park System established?
An act for the National Park Service was signed in 1916 but Yellowstone National Park was the first, established in 1872.
What is the largest national park?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve in Alaska.
What is the smallest national park?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial in Pennsylvania.
How many people visited national parks?
In 2020, there were 237 million visits.
How to make reservations for camping?
Most campsite reservations use recreation.gov
Can service dogs go to national parks?
Yes.

Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone is one of the top 5 national parks for the amount of visitors.
  • Yellowstone Lake is the largest high elevation lake in North America.
  • Has approximately 290 waterfalls throughout.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon is bigger than Rhode Island.
  • There is a town inside of the canyon.
  • It is full of ancient marine animal fossils.

Yosemite National Park

  • Yosemite supports over 400 species.
  • The only national park that has hosted the winter Olympics.
  • One of the few places that you can see a rainbow at night (also known as lunar rainbows).
